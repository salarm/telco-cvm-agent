# CVM Weekly Report — 2026-04-06 to 2026-04-12

## Executive Summary
26.89% acceptance across 1,127 assignments generated **$3,495.50 in revenue** from 282 revenue-generating customers — a solid viewed rate of 74.89%, but conversion is being dragged down by the Low value segment (16.57% acceptance on 31% of inventory) and an SMS channel that views well but closes poorly. The single most urgent action for next week is a targeted data add-on push to the **615 customers (61.5% of the base) projected to burn past 85% of their plan allowance** before cycle end — that pool represents the largest identifiable upsell and retention opportunity on the board.

---

## What Worked

1. **OFR_DT_ST_03 — standout offer of the week** — 57.14% acceptance rate (32/56 shown), more than double the campaign average of 26.89%, contributing $320.0 in revenue; this offer has demonstrated demand at scale and should be prioritised for expansion.

2. **High-value segment over-delivered** — 39.65% acceptance on 227 assignments, the strongest conversion of any value segment, generating $822.50 in revenue despite receiving only 20.1% of total assignments; revenue-per-assignment is $3.62 vs the $3.10 campaign average.

3. **Revenue concentration in top-5 offers is healthy** — OFR_DT_ST_04, OFR_DC_03, OFR_DC_05, OFR_DT_SA_04, and OFR_DT_ST_03 collectively produced **$2,324.75 (66.5% of total revenue)** from just 110 accepted instances, confirming that the DC/DT offer families are the commercial backbone.

---

## What Didn't

1. **Low segment is a conversion black hole** — 350 assignments (31.1% of all inventory) yielded only 16.57% acceptance — the worst of any segment and 10+ pp below the campaign mean — yet delivered $892.0, implying significant wasted impressions; reallocation to High/Premium (39.65% and 31.30% acceptance respectively) would improve efficiency materially.

2. **SMS views well but fails to close** — SMS had the highest viewed rate of the three channels (77.14%, 297/385 viewed) but the lowest acceptance rate (24.94%, 96 accepted), a 2.2 pp gap below in-app push (27.93%) and app banner (27.86%); the channel is generating attention without conversion, pointing to a CTA or offer-mix mismatch specific to SMS.

3. **Cashback offers (OFR_CB series) are low-yield** — The four CB offers average an acceptance rate of 33.4% but generate only $32.50–$78.75 in revenue each (total CB revenue ≈ $203.75, ~5.8% of total revenue); in contrast, DC offers average $390 per accepted offer; the CB series is consuming 146 assignment slots for minimal revenue return.

---

## Anomalies & Questions

- **OFR_DT_SA_01 and OFR_DT_SA_02 acceptance/revenue mismatch**: both offers carry strong acceptance rates (41.82% and 45.83%) but generate only $46.00 and $88.00 in revenue respectively — the lowest revenue-per-acceptance of any offer family. This may indicate a pricing error, a billing integration gap, or that these are zero-margin promotional SKUs being counted as conversions; finance and product should confirm.

- **61.5% burn-rate at-risk is unusually high**: 615 of 1,000 customers are projected to exceed 85% of plan limits before cycle end. If this is elevated versus prior weeks (benchmark data not available in this pull), it may signal a network event, school-holiday demand spike, or data quality issue in the projection model — worth validating against raw consumption logs before scaling next-week campaigns.

- **Premium segment under-served**: Premium received only 115 assignments (10.2% of total) despite a 31.30% acceptance rate and $293.0 in revenue; at the High segment's assignment level (227), Premium would be expected to generate an additional ~$290 in revenue. Allocation logic should be reviewed.
